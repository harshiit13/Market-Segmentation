AI : Bhagavad Gita (4.11): 'Ye yathā māṁ prapadyante tāṁs tathaiva bhajāmy aham, mama vartmānuvartante manuṣyāḥ sarva eva hi.' Meaning: Krishna assures that those who seek Him, irrespective of their paths, reach Him. Guidance: The Gita emphasizes that all sincere paths lead to the divine truth. Different religions are various expressions of the same spiritual quest. The essence of faith is devotion and righteousness, regardless of external rituals. Guidance: The Bhagavad Gita acknowledges and respects the diversity of spiritual paths, confirming that truth transcends religious boundaries. It emphasizes that devotion, faith, and righteous living are key to spiritual realization. In today's context, this teaches us to appreciate different religions as unique expressions of the same universal truth, promoting interfaith harmony.
